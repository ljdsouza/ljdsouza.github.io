I am a PhD candidate in the Control, Logic, and Learning (CL2) Lab at the University of Waterloo in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, co-supervised by Prof. Yash Pant and Prof. Sebastian Fischmeister. My research focuses on the design of controllers for hybrid (switched) systems, with an emphasis on data-driven and learning-based methods.

My MASc thesis in ECE at UW investigated learning Gaussian process models for use in mixed-integer optimization-based model predictive control. My current work builds on advances in model-free reinforcement learning to address the challenges posed by multi-modal dynamics governed by latent variables and the scalability of hybrid system control to high-dimensional state spaces. Broadly, my research interests span model-free RL, game-theoretic approaches to decision making, and generative modeling techniques for learning expressive and adaptable control policies, particularly in environments with uncertainty and limited prior knowledge.

Tractable Stochastic Hybrid Model Predictive Control using Gaussian Processes for Repetitive Tasks in Unseen Environments

Leroy D'Souza, Yash Vardhan Pant, Sebastian Fischmeister

European Control Conference (ECC), 2025
Paper Code

This work deals with chance-constrained model predictive control (MPC) for stochastic hybrid systems in environments where the switching locations are unknown apriori and can change over time. We build a mapping approach that iteratively improves performance in repetitive task settings and is capable of accounting for temporally changing mode distributions.
